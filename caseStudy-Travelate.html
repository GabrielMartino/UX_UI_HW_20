02 Research

5 User Interviews

The user interview process questions were designed to gather qualitative data to find out “What new considerations do users take into account when planning travel in a post-pandemic society”.

30 Surveys

For this survey I wanted to collect quantitative data that focused on the general information of a traveler. This allows me to discover the different demographics of travelers and quickly understand their basic preferences and lifestyles.
One particular interesting observation at a glance that was easy to identify was what travelers want when it comes to features in a travel app. Exposing the pain point of having to go to so many different websites and apps to successfully plan and manage a trip.

User Insights

What People Said

  • Majority of the user interviewed wanted to have an application that had everything they needed in one place.
  • They don't like to travel in peak season
  • Making lists is the best way to keep track of things
  • They value reviews from other people
  • Want someone to provide them with options / do the planning for them IE travel agent
  • A cost effective budget
We have observed that people with a busy lifestyle and budget constraints find it hard to spend their time and money efficiently. Because of this, they find it time consuming and burdensome to plan a trip that suits their preferences and requirements.

03 Definition

Problem Statement

We have observed that people with a busy lifestyle and budget constraints find it hard to spend their time and money efficiently. Because of this, they find it time consuming and burdensome to plan a trip that suits their preferences and requirements.

Good Qualities

Things to Avoid

Travelers need a new way to plan their trips with confidence. Our team has come together to define the current pain points of travel and develop a mobile app to help travelers navigate their planning journey.
  • Forced reading user agreements
  • Uncommon information gathering
  • Unclear and hard to read UI
  • Extra unnecessary steps

Brainstorming

People would find it easier to plan and manage their travel if they had a way to provide their travel parameters and have all their options that a relevant presented to them.

Ideas

  • We could make a kind of travel wizard that filters and sorts locations, activities, accommodation etc according to someone's budget, time they want to stay and activity preferences.
  • Similar to how some website builders have an automatic site builder from just selecting a couple of preferences and a template.

Concept

  • We could use a format that is similar to how people create accounts for social media to filter the content they see.
  • Using that method it will make it easier for people to store their travel information for future use as well.
We think this is a great opportunity to simplify a tedious process and to reassure travelers in their decisions.

Making an Impact

To make it easier for travelers to plan and budget by leading the user through a series of well defined steps to maximise their budget and time to create a fulfilling trip.

Assessing the Value

What we do

Travelate aims to provide it’s users with a simple and effective alternative to traditional travel planning. It allows time poor and budget conscious travelers to alleviate the burdens and restraints of planning, so that people can spend more time doing what matters to them.

How we do it

Travelate provides a simple and consolidated solution to travel planning designed to improve a travellers planning experience.

Why we do it

Adapting tried and tested methods from other major industries, Travelate innovates in the travel planning space, bringing you one step closer to your dream holiday.

Redefining the Journey

Storyboarding the Positive Outcome

1 - Initial Planning
Sandra is planning her trip to Manila using the Travelate website.
2 - Setting Preferences
She goes through the travel planning wizard and selects what she is interested in.
3 - Preference Notification
Shortly before her trip she receives a notification from the Travelate App about a planning festival near where she is to stay.
4 - Content Info / Issue
She has a look at the information and is really interested in going. But she notices it clashes with one of her planned activities!
5 - Rectification
She adds the festival to her activity list and shuffles around her itinerary to make room for it using the Travelate App.
6 - Successful Outcome
She is then able to go to the festival and still fit in all the other planned activities she wants to do.

06 Usability Testing

After creating wire frames I needed to test the app to validate the designed features and to inform a direction. I conducted 5 usability tests with the following tasks:
  • Can a user find what they want to do on their trip with the Travelate Wizard
  • The user successfully finds a location they like and allocates activities to certain days to build their itinerary
  • To see if the content is suitable as well as easy to input and edit
  • To see if selecting travel dates is easy for the user
  • To see if the content is suitable and if it is intuitive
  • To how users react to the results as well as how they use the selection tool

Test Results

LINK TO MIRO
The testing showed that I had gone in the wrong direction. Majority of the tasks were at 50% or lower success rate which lead me to gather more information on what the users were really wanting.

Testing Insights

LINK TO MIRO
The insights showed that my initial defining of features was poorly executed and needed to be re evaluated. Focusing on the key pain points, I started iterating on the design.

Iterations

AI Condensed, simplified and streamlined the searching and adding of places and activities to the users trip. It went from 4 disconnected screens to one intuitive search page.
  • Users wanted somewhere to search
  • There were too many tasks in a row in the wizard
  • Wanted more freedom of choice
  • Found it hard to navigate and keep track of where they were
  • The language used for headings and navigation was too vague
  • Too many irrelevant or unclear tasks

08 Final Thoughts

Sticking to the goal

Initially I was able to define a clear goal to work towards. But somewhere along the way I was veering off the path a lot and had to come back to the original goal, a travel PLANNING app, not a ‘trip generator’. Because of this I had to drastically change my original design. In doing this I gained a better perspective and appreciation of focusing on a goal.

Fail fast? Fail faster than light

I wish I had tested so much sooner and so much more often. If I had iterated a lot more on my design I would have been able to spend more time on creating improving the flow and function of the app a lot more.

Weaknesses

The thing I am most unhappy with about my project is the style. I feel like I really need to pay more attention to colours, shapes and typography. Another aspect in which I felt I struggled with was my lack of knowledge using Figma to prototype. After this project I am much more confident in my skills to make a usable prototype in a significantly shorter amount of time. On to the next one!
